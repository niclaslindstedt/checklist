# Archive

Some items you finish with but don't want to throw away. The **archive** is
where they go — out of your active list, but kept and recoverable, instead of
deleted.

A floating button on the left edge of the screen expands a navigation drawer
that switches between your current checklist and the **archive view** (see
[navigation](feature:navigation) for more on the drawer). The archive gathers
the archived items from **every** checklist into one place, grouped under a
heading for the list each item came from — so you can see at a glance what
you've set aside and where it belongs.

## How it works

1. To archive an item, **swipe right** on it in your list — it slides off into
   the archive rather than being destroyed (see [checklist](feature:checklist)).
   On a computer, **right-click** the item instead and choose **Archive** from
   the menu.
2. Open the navigation drawer and switch to the archive view.
3. Browse archived items grouped under a heading per source checklist; lists
   with nothing archived are left out.
4. On any archived item, choose **Restore** to send it back to the list it came
   from, or **Delete** to remove it for good. On a computer these live in the
   right-click menu; on touch they're buttons on the row.

Restore always returns an item to its **original** list, not whichever one
you're currently viewing, so nothing lands in the wrong place. Deleting from
the archive is permanent — it's the final step for an item you're truly done
with.

## Archiving a whole list

You can archive an **entire checklist**, not just its items. Right-click a list
in the navigation drawer (on a computer) and choose **Archive** — the whole
list leaves the switcher and moves to an **Archived lists** section at the top
of the archive view. From there, **Restore** brings it back to the switcher as
it was, or **Delete** removes it for good. The list you're currently looking at
can't be archived or deleted out from under you — there's always at least one
active list to show.
