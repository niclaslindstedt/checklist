# Undo and redo

Changed your mind, or deleted the wrong item? You can step back through your recent edits — and forward again — without losing anything. Undo and redo walk a timeline of the changes you've made to your [checklist](feature:checklist), restoring items you've deleted along the way.

## How it works

1. **Open the burger menu.** It now carries **Undo** and **Redo** entries near the foot of the list.
2. **Step back with Undo.** Each tap reverses the most recent change — un-deleting an item, undoing a rename, or putting back something you archived.
3. **Step forward with Redo.** If you undo one step too many, redo replays it.

You can also reach for the keyboard. `Cmd/Ctrl+Z` undoes the last change and `Cmd/Ctrl+Shift+Z` redoes it — the same steps as the menu, without leaving what you're doing.

Each time you step past a change, a brief toast names the action you just reversed — for example, `Undone: Deleted "milk"` — so you always know exactly what moved.
