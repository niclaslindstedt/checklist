# Getting around

`checklist` keeps your namespaces, lists, the [archive](feature:archive),
and undo/redo behind a single navigation menu. How you reach it depends on
how much room your screen has.

## The floating menu button

On phones the menu lives behind a **floating button**. Drag it and it
tracks your finger precisely, then **snaps to the nearest side of the
screen** and glides into place when you let go. It **remembers where you
left it**, so it stays on the edge you prefer across visits. Dragging it
downward will not accidentally trigger a pull-to-refresh, and when the
on-screen keyboard is up the button stays **above the
keyboard** where you can still reach it, instead of disappearing behind it.

Press the button and the menu slides in from whichever edge the button
rests on.

## Hiding the button

In the **installed app on Android or iOS**, you can turn the floating
button off entirely from the **General** settings. With it hidden, **swipe
in from the edge of the screen** to open the menu instead — a cleaner look
that trades the visible button for a gesture. This option only appears in
the installed app, where a swipe from the edge has room to work.

## The pinned sidebar

On wider screens — anything at least as wide as the smallest iPad — there
is no floating button and no gesture to learn. The navigation simply
**stays open as a permanent sidebar** beside your list, docked on the same
edge the button would have used. Your lists are always one click away,
right where you can see them.
